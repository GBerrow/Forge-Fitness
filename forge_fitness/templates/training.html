üí™ 4-Day Upper/Lower Split

Frequency: 2 upper body, 2 lower body days per week

üî• 5-Day Push/Pull/Legs Split

üìä Track Your Progress

Progress tracking is the bridge between effort and results. Without it, you're training blind ‚Äî unable to see patterns, identify what works, or know when to push harder or pull back. The good news? You don't need complex apps or spreadsheets. Simple, consistent tracking of your workouts, weights, and how you feel will give you the data you need to make informed decisions about your training.

Think of tracking as your fitness feedback loop. Every workout becomes a data point that helps you understand your body's response to training. Here's how to make it work for you:

Use the Built-in Notes System

Document your workouts, track weights, and note how you felt after each session using the notes section below.

Track Weights & Reps

Record your sets, reps, and weight for each exercise. Aim to gradually increase volume or intensity weekly.

Progressive Overload

Gradually increase weight, reps, or training volume every 1-2 weeks to ensure continued progress.

Consistency Tracking

Mark your completed workouts and aim for 80% adherence to your chosen plan for optimal results.
